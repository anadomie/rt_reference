Skills

Name Characteristic Type Description Special Use
Acrobatics Agility Full Action A character with the Acrobatics skill can maintain balance or avoid falling. It allows them to jump to or from a height and navigate obstacles without falling.
  • Contortionist: Can use his Acrobatics skill to escape from bonds as a Full Action. May also escape from a grapple by using their Acrobatics skill instead of their Agility or Strength when grappling.
  • Manoeuvring: The character may make a Disengage action as a Half Action if he passes a Challenging (+0) Acrobatics skill test.
  • Jumping: When jumping or leaping, may use the Acrobatics skill in the place of AG and S tests. When determining Falling damage, can make an Acrobatics skill test to reduce the effective distance fallen by a number of metres equal to his AGB.
Athletics Strength Full Action A character with the Athletics skill can swim across water, push beyond their physical limits, or climb difficult terrain. This skill helps overcome challenging physical obstacles and demonstrates endurance.
  • Endurance: As a Free Action, can make a Dificult (-10) Athletics skill test whenever he suffers a level of Fatigue as a result of running, swimming, or climbing to ignore its effects. They can continue to take tests and ignore Fatigue gained indefinitely; however, if a test fails, they must rest for at least 4 hours and cannot use his Athletics skill again during that time.
  • Heft: As a Free Action, a character can make a Challenging (+0) Athletics skill test to increase his carrying capability for a single encounter. For every degree of success, Strength is increased by 10 for how much weight can carry, lift, or drag, to a max S of 100. Hefting can only be attempted once per encounter.
Awareness Perception Free Action A character with the Awareness skill can spot ambushes, search for clues or hidden items, and eavesdrop on conversations. This skill is useful for noticing hidden details in the environment and gaining a tactical advantage.
  • Lip Reading: Dificulty of this test is modified -10 for every 10 metres of distance between the character and the speaker. A successful Awareness skill test reveals the general gist of the conversation.
Charm Fellowship 1 minute A character with the Charm skill can improve someone's opinion of them, distract others by drawing their focus away, or gather information from locals and strangers. This skill is useful for influencing social interactions and acquiring valuable information.
  • Target makes a Willpower save against Charm's characteristic test.
Command Fellowship Half for Simple, Full for complex A character with the Command skill can send an allied NPC into an extremely dangerous situation or open combat. They can also rally allied fighting forces and organize them in the face of a surprise attack. This skill is crucial for coordinating troops and maintaining morale and discipline.
  • Inspire: As a Full Action, the character can make a Challenging (+0) Command test to inspire one or more of his followers. Inspired followers receive a +10 on their next skill or characteristic test.
  • Terrify: As a Reaction, try to counter the effects of Fear in his allies should they fail their WP test. Make a Command skill test opposed by the WP of the Fear-causing creature. Both the character and the creature add +10 to their rolls for each level of Fear they possess. If the character is successful, his allies ignore the effects of Fear
Commerce Intelligence 1 minute, or more for complex deals A character with the Commerce skill can gain a bonus when attempting a Requisition test, try to track down a rare item, or ascertain the value of an item. This skill is useful for efficiently acquiring resources and gaining trade advantages.
  • Target makes a Commerce or Willpower save to oppose.
  • Evaluate: A success tells the character the Availability of the item he is evaluating, while a failure tells him nothing. If he fails by more than 3 degrees of failure, the GM should give a false Availability.
Common Lore Intelligence Free Action A character with the Common Lore skill can learn about a situation, location, character, or object, subject to their specialization. This skill allows them to gain well-founded knowledge about various subjects and draw useful conclusions.
Deceive Fellowship 1 minute, or more for complex deception A character with the Deceive skill can tell a convincing lie, distract someone with rambling nonsense, or try to disguise themselves. This skill is useful for manipulating others, avoiding pursuit, or blending into a crowd.
  • Disguise: Can create a disguise, given time and raw materials. The time taken depends greatly on the complexity of the disguise. Additional deception checks may be necessary as one comes under scrutiny.
Demolitions Intelligence Variable Success on a Demolitions skill test indicates a character has successfully planted an explosive charge, and set a trigger of his choice. Defusing charges also falls under the Demolitions skill, with an Opposed test against the Demolitions skill test of the individual who set the explosives. Winning the test defuses the bomb. Simple failure means that while the charge has not been disarmed, neither has it gone off. Four or more degrees of failure indicate the character has set off the device, suffering the consequences of the explosion.
Dodge Agility Reaction A character with the Dodge skill can evade a melee or ranged attack or avoid hazards. This skill is crucial for avoiding damage and enhancing survivability in dangerous situations.
  • Dive for Cover: If there is cover with a number of metres equal to the character's AGB and they are not Prone, they can Dive for Cover as a Reaction against a ranged attack instead of trying to Evade. This requires an Ordinary (+10) Dodge test; if successful, the character leaps behind cover and goes Prone. They are still hit by the attack, but benefit from the AP of the Cover.
Forbidden Lore Intelligence Free Action A character with the Forbidden Lore skill can seek information about potential cult activity, learn about the operations of xenos, or determine the proper procedure to summon a daemon. This skill allows them to gain deep and often dangerous knowledge about forbidden subjects.
Inquiry Fellowship One hour or more A character with the Inquiry skill can try to track down a local crime lord, attempt to learn the attitudes of the local populace, or gather information about specific locations. This skill is useful for acquiring targeted knowledge about people, places, or social structures.
Interrogation Willpower One hour or more A character with the Interrogation skill can attempt to learn battle plans from a commander, force an Imperial Adept to reveal hidden information, or have a cultist disclose a secret meeting place. This skill is useful for extracting crucial information through focused questioning and pressure.
Intimidate Strength Full Action A character with the Intimidate skill can coerce someone into acting a certain way or try to make an opponent back down from a fight. This skill is useful for exerting control through fear and resolving conflicts without direct violence.
Linguistics Intelligence Free Action A character with the Linguistics skill can decipher an archaic text, compose a moving piece of prose, or convey a message with limited vocabulary. This skill is useful for understanding complex or foreign languages and communicating effectively.
Logic Intelligence 1 minute, or more for complex problem A character with the Logic skill can solve a riddle, look for clues in vast amounts of unrelated information, or try to win a game of chance. This skill is useful for applying analytical thinking and making rational decisions.
  • Gambling: In a typical game, each participant wagers an amount, though these are typically the same, and makes an Opposed test with the Logic skill. The character with the most degrees of success or fewest degrees of failure wins the pot.
  • Ciphers and Decoding: A character can use the Logic skill to unravel ciphers and codes. This usually takes a great deal of time. In general, these tests should be Difficult (-10) at the easiest, unless the cipher is already partially decoded or the character has a key available to aid his efforts.
Medicae Intelligence Full Action A character with the Medicae skill can apply first aid to a wounded ally, diagnose an affliction and develop a treatment, or perform a surgical procedure for the removal of an organ or the implantation of a cybernetic. This skill is essential for providing medical care and life-saving interventions.
  • First Aid: make a +0 Medicae test, with a -10 penalty if Heavily Damaged or a -10 penalty for every point of Critical damage. If successful, remove damage equal to IB, plus one point of damage per DoS on the test (removing Critical damage before normal damage). A character can only be treated with first aid once every 24 hours, and only when not also under extended care.
  • Extended Care: Can treat paitents equal to IB, plus one per assistant. For each patient beyond this number, he suffers a -10 penalty on Medicae tests. For each assistant trained in the Medicae skill, gain +10. At the end of each 24-hour period of extended care, the primary healer performs a (+10) Medicae test. If successful, patients heal double the wounds as with their natural rate of healing.
  • Diagnose: A successful skill test yields the name of the ailment and basic treatments. When used on groups, a successful skill test can prevent the spread of disease or mitigate the effects of malnutrition.
Navigate (Surface or Stellar or Warp) Intelligence 1 minute for simple location; 1d5 hours for plotting courses or routes. A character with the Navigation skill can make a journey across unfamiliar terrain, find their bearings in a strange place, or create a map showing the quickest route from one point to another. This skill is crucial for safe travel and route planning in unfamiliar areas.
  • Surface: Make a journey across unfamiliar terrain, find one's bearings in a strange place, or create a map showing the quickest route from one point to another.
  • Stellar: Make a journey within a star system, find a ship stranded in an unknown region of space, or find the quickest route from one location within a system (such as a planet) to another location in the system.
  • Warp: Guide a starship through the Warp, or convert a journey into a Warp route chart
Operate (Surface or Aeronautica or Voidship) Agility Half Action A character with the Operate skill can drive a vehicle in combat, push it beyond its normal limits, or attempt to maneuver through a crowded highway. This skill is essential for controlling vehicles safely and effectively under challenging conditions.
  • Surface: Drive a vehicle in combat, push a vehicle beyond its normal limits, or attempt to manoeuvre though a crowded highway.
  • Aeronautica: Pilot an aero in a combat situation, fly beyond its limit, or try to land on difficult terrain.
  • Voidship: Manoeuvre a voidship or oversee the use of a macrobattery, orbital laser, or ship's weaponry.
Parry Weapon Skill Reaction A character with the Parry skill can attempt to deflect blows in melee combat. This skill is crucial for intercepting attacks and minimizing incoming damage.
Psyniscience Perception Half Action A character with the Psyniscience skill can hunt for the location of daemons, detect the effects of psychic power, or determine a thin point in the veil between the Materium and the Warp. This skill is essential for dealing with supernatural threats and psychic phenomena.
Scholastic Lore Intelligence Free Action A character with the Scholastic Lore skill can recount legends from the past, understand an obscure law, or identify a rare beast. This skill is useful for gaining deep knowledge in specialized fields and making informed decisions.
Scrutiny Intelligence Half Action A character with the Scrutiny skill can tell if someone is lying, collate information and gather conclusions from a report, or interpret augur or auspex readings. This skill is valuable for making informed decisions and uncovering deception.
Security Intelligence 1 minute, reduced by 10 seconds for each degree of success. A character with the Security skill can open a locked door or break into a cogitator to discover its secrets. This skill is crucial for bypassing security measures and accessing restricted information.
  • Traps: Can fashion and set traps. The time and resources required, and damage caused by, these kinds of traps depend on the GM's discretion and the intended complexity and effects of the traps.
Sleight of Hand Agility Half Action, or can be performed as a Free Action with an additional -10 penalty. A character with the Sleight of Hand skill can attempt to steal something from a target's pocket, palm evidence at a crime scene, or plant illegal narcotics into a suspect's robes. This skill is essential for subtle manipulations and covert actions.
Stealth Agility Free Action A character with the Stealth skill can scout an enemy force without being detected, sneak up on a foe and kill them quietly, or follow someone without arousing suspicion. This skill is essential for covert operations and discreet movement.
  • Shadowing: Stealth can be used to shadow others and follow them unseen, either on foot, in vehicles, or even in shuttles and starships. Stealth skill tests for shadowing are always Opposed tests against the opponent's Awareness. A single Stealth skill test is sufficient to follow the opponent unseen for 5 minutes per degree of success.
Survival Perception Full Action A character with the Survival skill can find food or water in a hostile environment, start a fire with homemade tools, or track a foe across a hive. This skill is crucial for enduring extreme conditions and navigating challenging environments.
  • Crafting: Survival can be used to make all kinds of primitive devices, from cloaks and stone buildings to spears and armour. Tracking: A character can use the Survival skill to follow the signs left by his quarry. A cunning or devious quarry might attempt to conceal or erase any tracks; in cases where an opponent has done this, the tracker should make an Opposed test against the prey's Stealth.
  • Wrangling: Characters can tame, train, and potentially ride animals encountered in their travels. Both taming and training beasts are Extended tests, the duration of which is determined by the nature and temperament of the creature involved. Wrangling is of no use against cyber or psy-bonded animals, nor against any creatures with true sentience.
Tech-Use Intelligence 1 minute, or more for more complex systems. A character with the Tech-Use skill can repair a broken weapon or vehicle, push a piece of technology beyond its normal limits, or determine how to use unknown technology. This skill is essential for maintaining, modifying, and understanding technical equipment.
  • Crafting: can be used to make technical devices, from simple clockwork and steam engines to advanced weaponry and armour. The time, difficulty, and materials it takes to create these items are greatly dependent on their complexity.
Trade Intelligence Variable depending on task and complexity. A character with the Trade skill can prepare a meal for high-ranking officers, carve their sigil on a chainsword, or uncover the remains of a lost colony. This skill is useful for specialized craftsmanship and creative tasks.
  • Trade has multiple specializations; these are detailed on the character sheet.
